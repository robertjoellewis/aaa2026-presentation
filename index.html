The Superstimuli Framework

Entertainment is
Attention Technology

Entertainment is not random. Animated characters get cuter. Monsters get scarier. Romantic leads more idealized. Editing faster. This is a directed evolutionary process.

Entertainment is a deliberately iterated technological system of superstimuli: unnaturally attention-grabbing stimuli that tap into evolved psychological mechanisms more intensely than natural stimuli.

Lewis (under review) · Journal of Media Psychology

Tinbergen's gulls preferred fake eggs with bigger spots over their own real eggs. Audiences prefer fictional threats that are bigger, villains more menacing, romances more idealized than anything in their actual lives.

The key insight: Producers have been building superstimuli by trial and error for centuries. Theory tells us which evolved systems they're exploiting. And that tells us what to measure.

Implications for Education

Three Curricular
Recommendations

1

Teach psychometrics alongside AI tools

Students using AI for audience research need to understand latent constructs, measurement validity, and factor structure. Without this, they use AI to measure the wrong things more efficiently, at scale, with confidence.

2

Give students unified theory, not just fragmented findings

The superstimuli framework subsumes character research, cultivation research, and body image research under one organizing mechanism. Instead of three separate literatures, students see the underlying unity and can apply AI across all of them simultaneously.

3

Create AI + methods integration courses (not AI courses)

The skill to build: given an industry question, identify the psychological construct, select the measurement approach, deploy AI to operationalize and scale it.
